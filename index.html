Concepts From the Book Cover

Synthetic Oblivion: A drug of forgetting; produced not by chemistry, but by endless digital feeds that drown memory in infinite novelty.

Cybernetic Yearning: Desire shaped by feedback loops; wanting only what is most ‘liked’, clicked, viewed, followed, and optimized; and therefore the least authentic.

Prosthetic Consciousness: Author Marshall McLuhan foresaw the electronic medium as an extension of human nervous systems, but what has emerged in the early 21st Century is a prosthetic consciousness; owned, curated, and manipulated by the very few.

Cognitive Abyss: The vertigo of realizing that thought itself is derivative, assembled from fragments of algorithmically surfaced content.
